पूज्य ऋषिवर श्री किरीटभाई जी

The vakta whose voice carried Sankhya Shastra into lakhs of homes in December 2006.

Pujya Rishivar Shri Kiritbhai Ji is revered across India and abroad as a marmagya — a knower of the inner heart — of Shrimad Bhagavat and Shri Ramcharitmanas. For decades his kathas, marked by a uniquely sweet voice, deep shastric grounding and an unbroken stream of bhava and bhakti, have drawn audiences in the lakhs, both in person and through television channels such as Aastha and Sanskar.

What set the December 2006 telecast apart was the subject. Kathas of Bhagavat and Manas are heard everywhere; a sustained, day-wise exposition of Sankhya Shastra on live national television was — and remains — rare. Bhaiji approached it not as dry philosophy but through the Bhagavata's own door: the dialogue in which Bhagwan Kapila, the avatara who is Sankhya's founding acharya, teaches his mother Devahuti. Tattva became katha; enumeration became darshan.

Editor's slot: add here your personal recollections of the 2006 telecast — the venue, the yajman parivar, the bhajan mandali, and the moments that stayed with you. First-hand memory is the most precious content this page can hold.

Seeking the original recordings

The full DVD set of this satsang has not surfaced in public online archives. Two avenues are most likely to succeed:

  • Aastha Channel archives — Aastha Broadcasting Network Ltd. retains masters of its telecasts; a written request quoting the speaker, subject and telecast dates (first half of December 2006) is the right approach.
  • The Acharya's own organisation — Pujya Kiritbhai Ji's katha media is today managed through his official channels (search "Rishivar Shri Kiritbhai Ji" on YouTube for current contact points); older kathas were distributed on DVD by the organisers, who may hold remaining stock or masters.

Any recordings recovered can be dropped into each day's media slots on this site — the pages are ready to receive them.
