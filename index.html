यह संग्रह · About this archive

Why this satsang deserves to be remembered

In the first half of December 2006, Pujya Shri Kiritbhai Ji took up a subject few kathakars attempt on live television: Sankhya Shastra — the oldest of India's six darshanas, the philosophy Bhagwan Shri Krishna himself invokes in the second chapter of the Gita. For seven days on Aastha Channel, the driest-sounding of subjects — tattvas, gunas, the count of the principles of existence — came alive through katha, bhajan and the Kapila–Devahuti samvad of Shrimad Bhagavat. This site preserves that experience day by day.

एषा तेऽभिहिता साङ्ख्ये बुद्धिर्योगे त्विमां शृणु ।
बुद्ध्या युक्तो यया पार्थ कर्मबन्धं प्रहास्यसि ॥
"This wisdom of Sankhya has been declared to you, O Partha; now hear the wisdom of Yoga, endowed with which you shall cast off the bondage of karma."
श्रीमद्भगवद्गीता २.३९
A note on the contents: the day-wise teachings below present the subject matter of the satsang — Sankhya Shastra as expounded in the tradition of Maharshi Kapila, the Sankhya Karika and the Kapila–Devahuti samvad — arranged as it unfolds over a seven-day katha. The verbatim transcript, bhajans and video clips of the original 2006 telecast are being added to each day's page from the archive recordings as they are recovered.
दिन-प्रतिदिन · Day by day

The seven days of the satsang

Sankhya means "enumeration" — the discipline of counting reality's principles until only the Witness remains uncounted. The seven days follow that very movement: from the fact of suffering, to the two eternals, to the twenty-five tattvas, to bondage, to bhakti, to liberation.

Pujya Shri Kiritbhai Ji

Rishivar Shri Kiritbhai Ji is revered as a marmagya — a knower of the heart — of Shrimad Bhagavat and Ramcharitmanas, whose kathas have moved audiences across India and abroad for decades.
